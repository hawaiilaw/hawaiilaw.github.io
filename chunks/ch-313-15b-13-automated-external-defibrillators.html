§ 15B-13.1 Definitions.
   For the purposes of this article, the following definitions apply unless the context clearly indicates or requires a different meaning.
Automated External Defibrillator. A portable electronic device, approved by the United States Food and Drug Administration, that is capable of diagnosing cardiac arrhythmias and that uses defibrillation to reestablish an effective cardiac rhythm.
Revenue Service. The time when a train is available to carry passengers and provide transit service. Trains operated in fare-free service are considered in revenue service. Revenue service excludes train testing and maintenance.
Train. Four passenger cars coupled together and propelled by self-contained motor equipment to provide transit service on a fixed rail guideway system operated by the city.
(Added by Ord. 21-10; Am. Ord. 22-18 )
§ 15B-13.2 Requirement to provide automated external defibrillators.
   Every train in revenue service, as defined in this article, must have installed and maintained therein automated external defibrillators in accordance with the requirements of this article. At least two automated external defibrillators that are accessible to employees and the public, as applicable, must be placed in each train in revenue service. Appropriate signage must be posted to indicate the location of the automated external defibrillators.
(Added by Ord. 21-10; Am. Ord. 22-18 )
§ 15B-13.3 Administering agency.
   The department of transportation services or another city agency designated by the mayor shall administer this article.
(Added by Ord. 21-10; Am. Ord. 22-18 )
§ 15B-13.4 Notification.
   The administering agency shall notify the Honolulu emergency services department, emergency medical services division, of the existence, location, and type of automated external defibrillator installed in each train.
(Added by Ord. 21-10; Am. Ord. 22-18 )
§ 15B-13.5 Maintenance.
   The administering agency shall conduct periodic inspections of all automated external defibrillators that have been installed in trains to ensure that they are maintained in an operative condition, and are replaced or repaired where defective, all according to the operation and maintenance guidelines set forth by the manufacturer.
(Added by Ord. 21-10; Am. Ord. 22-18 )
