Sections

§ 34-17.1 Definitions.
   For the purposes of this article, the following definitions apply unless the context clearly indicates or requires a different meaning.
Plastic. Any material made of fossil fuel-derived or petrochemical polymeric compounds and additives that can be shaped by flow.
Plastic Bottle. A formed or molded container made from any type of plastic having a relatively inflexible fixed shape or form with a neck that is smaller than the container body.
Single-Use. An item that is designed to be discarded by the user after a single or limited number of uses and not designed or manufactured for long-term or multiple re-use.
(Added by Ord. 25-18)
§ 34-17.2 Restrictions on single-use plastic bottles.
   No single-use plastic bottle having a capacity of 1 liter or less may be sold or offered for sale at any vending machine located at a city facility after December 31, 2025. Any contracts in effect prior to the enactment of this article may be fulfilled and any subsequent contract renewals must be executed in compliance with this article.
(Added by Ord. 25-18)
§ 34-17.3 Exemptions.
   Vendors participating in the Licensed Blind Vendor program, which is also known as the Vending Facilities program of the Randolph-Sheppard Act of 1936, 20 USC § 107 et. seq. and as amended in 1954 and 1974, are exempted from this article.
(Added by Ord. 25-18)
