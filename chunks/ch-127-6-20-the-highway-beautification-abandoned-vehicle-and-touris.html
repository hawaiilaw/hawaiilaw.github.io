TOURISM-RELATED TRAFFIC CONGESTION REVOLVING FUND

§ 6-20.1 Creation.
   There is created and established a special fund to be known as the “highway beautification, abandoned vehicle, and tourism-related traffic congestion revolving fund.”
(Sec. 5-25.1, R.O. 1978 (1983 Ed.)) (1990 Code, Ch. 6, Art. 20, § 6-20.1) (Am. Ord. 25-37)
§ 6-20.2 Purpose.
There will be deposited into the highway beautification, abandoned vehicle, and tourism-related traffic congestion revolving fund all receipts from the fee permitted under HRS § 286-51. The fee assessed and collected will be $10 for motor vehicles.
All moneys deposited into the highway beautification, abandoned vehicle, and tourism-related traffic congestion revolving fund must be expended in accordance with HRS § 286-51 for:
(1)   Beautification and other related activities of primary highways under the ownership, control, and jurisdiction of the city;
(2)   Disposition and other related activities of abandoned vehicles as prescribed in HRS Chapter 290; and
(3)   Activities to mitigate and address the impacts of tourism-related traffic congestion.
(Sec. 5-25.2, R.O. 1978 (1987 Supp. to 1983 Ed.)) (1990 Code, Ch. 6, Art. 20, § 6-20.2) (Am. Ords. 02-30, 03-13, 09-16, 25-37)
§ 6-20.3 Expenditures.
   All expenditures from the highway beautification, abandoned vehicle, and tourism-related traffic congestion revolving fund must be for purposes authorized herein, based on appropriations in the operating budget or capital budget ordinance, or both.
(Sec. 5-25.3, R.O. 1978 (1983 Ed.)) (1990 Code, Ch. 6, Art. 20, § 6-20.3) (Am. Ord. 25-37)
§ 6-20.4 Administration.
   The director of budget and fiscal services and the chief budget officer shall be responsible for the administration of the highway beautification, abandoned vehicle, and tourism-related traffic congestion revolving fund. Expenditures from the highway beautification, abandoned vehicle, and tourism-related traffic congestion revolving fund must be in accordance with prescribed laws and procedures applicable to expenditures of city revenues.
(Sec. 5-25.4, R.O. 1978 (1983 Ed.)) (1990 Code, Ch. 6, Art. 20, § 6-20.4) (Am. Ord. 25-37)
