ARTICLE 9: AUTHORITY OF EXECUTIVE AGENCY TO ADOPT RULES

Sections
   1-9.1   Adoption of rules

§ 1-9.1 Adoption of rules.
   The head of any executive agency whose power or function as prescribed by law directly affects the public, may adopt rules having force and effect of law pursuant to HRS Chapter 91, setting forth procedures that are necessary for such agency in dealing with the public concerning such power or function.
(Sec. 1-10.1, R.O. 1978 (1983 Ed.)) (1990 Code, Ch. 1, Art. 9, § 1-9.1)
§ 1-9.2 Format of rules.
(a)   The department of the corporation counsel shall prescribe a uniform format for the preparation and publication of the rules of the executive departments and agencies of the city. The uniform format must provide that each rule published will be accompanied by a reference to the authority pursuant to which the rule has been adopted, the law implemented by the rule, if any, and the effective date of the rule. The uniform format must further provide that, whenever possible, applicable law should be incorporated by reference and not be reprinted in the rule.
(b)   If a uniform format is no longer prescribed pursuant to subsection (a), the rules of the executive departments and agencies of the city must be prepared and published in the form prescribed in the most recent edition of the Hawaii Administrative Rules Drafting Manual prepared by the State legislative reference bureau; provided that references therein to the Hawaii Revised Statutes will be deemed references to the Revised Ordinances of Honolulu, when appropriate, and other such adjustments will be made as necessary to give the drafting manual a reasonable interpretation when applied to the city.
(Sec. 1-10.2, R.O. 1978 (1987 Supp. to 1983 Ed.)) (1990 Code, Ch. 1, Art. 9, § 1-9.2) (Am. Ord. 25-9)
