§ 6-55.1 Establishment.
   There is created a special fund to be known as the “transit construction mitigation fund.”
(1990 Code, Ch. 6, Art. 55, § 6-55.1) (Added by Ord. 15-37)
§ 6-55.2 Purpose—Definitions.
(a)   The purpose of the transit construction mitigation fund is to receive and expend moneys to mitigate negative economic impacts from the construction of the Rail Project.
(b)   For the purposes of this article, the following definitions apply unless the context clearly indicates or requires a different meaning:
Business. Any sole proprietorship, partnership, joint venture, business trust, limited liability company, corporation, or other business entity.
City Block. The smallest area designated on an official map of the city that is bounded on all sides by intersecting public streets.
Rail Project. The Honolulu High-Capacity Transit Corridor Project.
(1990 Code, Ch. 6, Art. 55, § 6-55.2) (Added by Ord. 15-37; Am. Ord. 24-1)
§ 6-55.3 Deposits.
   Only moneys from the general fund or other federal, State, or private sources that have been so specified for these purposes may be deposited into the transit construction mitigation fund.
(1990 Code, Ch. 6, Art. 55, § 6-55.3) (Added by Ord. 15-37)
§ 6-55.4 Expenditures.
   Expenditures from the transit construction mitigation fund may only be used to mitigate negative economic impacts on businesses from the Rail Project and to provide outreach to businesses under § 6-55.7, provided that:
(1)   Expenditures may not be used to mitigate beyond impacts from construction of the Rail Project;
(2)   Expenditures per eligible business may not exceed $10,000 per fiscal year;
(3)   Not more than 5 percent of the moneys deposited into the transit construction mitigation fund may be used to administer this article by the director of budget and fiscal services under § 6-55.6; and
(4)   Not more than 5 percent of the moneys deposited into the transit construction mitigation fund may be used to conduct outreach activities by the agency designated by the mayor under § 6-55.7.
(1990 Code, Ch. 6, Art. 55, § 6-55.4) (Added by Ord. 15-37; Am. Ord. 24-1)
§ 6-55.5 Criteria.
(a)   To qualify for an expenditure under § 6-55.4, an applicant must, at a minimum:
(1)   Be a business that is majority-owned by city residents and that does business with customers primarily at a physical location located within a city block in which there is active construction on the Rail Project at the time of application;
(2)   Generate $1,000,000 or less in annual revenue; and
(3)   Have opened for business at least 12 months prior to the start of any Rail Project construction within the transit construction mitigation zone in which the business is physically located, as determined by the director of budget and fiscal services.
(b)   The director of budget and fiscal services may establish additional selection criteria for applicants under this section, including determinations on applicant viability.
(Added by Ord. 24-1) (Am. Ord. 25-35)
§ 6-55.6 Administration—Rules.
(a)   The director of budget and fiscal services shall administer the transit construction mitigation fund.
(b)   The director of budget and fiscal services shall adopt rules pursuant to HRS Chapter 91 to implement, enforce, and administer this article, including rules regarding:
(1)   Application procedures;
(2)   Deadlines;
(3)   Expenditure amounts; and
(4)   Additional selection criteria
for grants and loans funded with transit construction mitigation fund moneys.
(c)   Expenditures from the transit construction mitigation fund must be in accordance with prescribed laws and procedures applicable to the expenditure of city revenues.
(Added by Ord. 24-1)
§ 6-55.7 Outreach.
   The mayor shall designate an agency to conduct outreach activities with businesses that may be eligible to receive grants or loans under this article. The outreach activities may include:
(1)   Providing public notice in a daily newspaper or an electronic medium, such as the internet, or both;
(2)   Directly contacting potentially eligible applicants, including by canvassing; and
(3)   Providing assistance to eligible applicants throughout the application process.
(Added by Ord. 24-1)
